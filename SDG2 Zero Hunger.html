SDG 2 Zero Hunger

What is SDG 2?

SDG 2 aims to end hunger, achieve food security, improve nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture. By 2030, the goal is to ensure that everyone has access to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food all year round.

Hunger is a global crisis affecting millions of people, particularly in developing countries. It not only causes immense suffering but also hinders social and economic development.

To achieve SDG 2, we must address key issues such as:

  • Food security and access to nutritious food
  • Malnutrition, especially among children and vulnerable populations
  • Sustainable agriculture and responsible food production
  • Reducing food waste and promoting efficient supply chains

By prioritizing sustainable and inclusive food systems, we can make significant progress towards ending hunger and ensuring a healthier and more prosperous future for all.

IMPORTANCE OF SDG-2       

  • Human health and well-being: Everyone has the right to access safe, nutritious, and sufficient food.
  • Social justice: Hunger and malnutrition disproportionately affect the poorest and most vulnerable people in society. SDG-2 can help to reduce inequality and promote social justice.
  • Peace and security: Hunger and malnutrition can lead to social unrest and conflict. SDG-2 can help to create a more peaceful and secure world.

How India is implementing SDG 2

The National Food Security Act (NFSA):* The NFSA guarantees food security to all citizens of India. The act provides subsidized food grains to eligible households through the Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS).

The Mid-Day Meal Scheme:* The Mid-Day Meal Scheme provides free cooked meals to children in primary schools. The scheme aims to improve the nutritional status of children and reduce school dropout rates.

The Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY):* The PMKSY is a major irrigation program that aims to provide water for irrigation to all farmers in India. The program is expected to improve agricultural productivity and reduce hunger.

The National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA):* The NMSA is a program that aims to promote sustainable agriculture in India. The program focuses on improving soil health, increasing crop diversification, and reducing the use of pesticides.
